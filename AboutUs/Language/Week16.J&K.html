📍 Situation: You're at a ramen restaurant. You want to order, ask about spice, and request the bill.
Staff
いらっしゃいませ！何名様ですか？
Irasshaimase! Nan-mei-sama desu ka?
Welcome! How many people?
You
一人です。すみません、醤油ラーメンをひとつお願いします。辛くしないでください。
Hitori desu. Sumimasen, shōyu rāmen wo hitotsu onegaishimasu. Karakunishinai de kudasai.
Just me. Excuse me, one soy sauce ramen please. Please don't make it spicy.
Staff
かしこまりました。少々お待ちください。
Kashikomarimashita. Shōshō omachi kudasai.
Understood. Please wait a moment.
You (after eating)
すみません！お会計をお願いします。とてもおいしかったです！
Sumimasen! Okaikei wo onegaishimasu. Totemo oishikatta desu!
Excuse me! The bill please. It was really delicious!
Grammar used: 〜です (W3) · 〜をひとつお願いします (W6) · て-form + ください (W9) · 〜かった (W14 past tense)

📍 Situation: You're at a Korean BBQ restaurant. You want to order samgyeopsal, ask for less spice, and request the bill.
Staff
어서오세요! 몇 분이세요?
Eoseooseyo! Myeot buniseyo?
Welcome! How many people?
You
두 명이요. 삼겹살 2인분 주세요. 그리고 덜 맵게 해주실 수 있어요?
Du myeong-iyo. Samgyeopsal i-inbun juseyo. Geurigo deol maepge haejusil su isseoyo?
Two people. Please give us 2 portions of samgyeopsal. And could you make it less spicy?
Staff
네, 알겠습니다! 반찬도 더 필요하시면 말씀해 주세요.
Ne, algessseumnida! Banchanjo deo piryohasimyeon malsseum-hae juseyo.
Yes, understood! If you need more banchan, please let us know.
You (after eating)
저기요! 계산해 주세요. 정말 맛있었어요!
Jeogiyo! Gyesan-hae juseyo. Jeongmal masisseoyo!
Excuse me! The bill please. It was really delicious!
Grammar used: 두 명이요 (W4) · 주세요 (W2) · 덜 맵게 해주실 수 있어요 (W11 potential) · -(으)면 (W9) · 맛있었어요 (W6)

Your Next Steps — Where to Go From Here

Recommended next steps — both languages
You're now an elementary learner — here's what to do next
📱
Daily practice apps
Duolingo or LingoDeer for JP · Duolingo for KR. 10–15 minutes daily is worth more than 2 hours once a week. Consistency is everything.
Free to start
📺
Passive immersion
Watch Japanese anime or Korean dramas with subtitles. Start with subtitles in English, progress to subtitles in the target language. Listening ear develops fastest through immersion.
Netflix / Viki / Crunchyroll
🎌
Japanese: Complete hiragana & kanji
Finish learning all 46 hiragana and 46 katakana if not done. Then begin the JLPT N5 kanji list (100 characters). Wanikani app makes kanji systematic and memorable.
Next milestone: JLPT N5
🇰🇷
Korean: Complete Hangul mastery
You can read Hangul — now build reading speed. Read menus, signs, and subtitles. Korean vocabulary expands very fast because Hangul is fully phonetic.
Next milestone: TOPIK I
💬
Find a language exchange partner
Apps like HelloTalk, Tandem, or Speaky connect you with native speakers who want to learn English. Real conversation is the fastest accelerator after this foundation course.
HelloTalk / Tandem
📖
Get a textbook for each language
JP: Genki I (highly recommended, used in universities worldwide). KR: Talk To Me in Korean Level 1 (free online + paid workbook). Both are excellent, structured, and fun.
Genki · TTMIK
✈️
Plan a trip!
You now have enough to travel independently in Japan and Korea. Ordering food, navigating transport, checking into hotels, talking to locals — you can do all of it. Go and use what you've learned.
The best classroom is the country
